Many times, wherever I am, I can feel that I’m being treated as if I’m a child. It really only happens with people who don’t know me yet and only scratch the surface. I’ve indeed got a younger look; I’m sure I would be able to lie about my age anywhere I wanted to. Even my driving school thinks I’m too young; they told my parents “Kids these days only look at their phones, and they can’t follow rules”. Maybe it’s true for my generation. Yet I feel like the complete opposite. Don’t get me wrong; it’s quite awkward and unnecessary to say things like “I’m different” or “I was born in the wrong generation.” No, I’m exactly in the right place at the right time. I also hate when people build their brands around the idea that they’re “young entrepreneurs.” No one cares how old you are; it literally makes zero difference. You don’t see “old entrepreneur” or “middle-aged” either. “Kids these days…”

Don’t judge people by their age.
