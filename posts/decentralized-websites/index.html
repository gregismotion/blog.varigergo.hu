Back when I was a young(er) kid, I remember that I always wanted to make a game. I was always ambitious—AAA Games, Open World, TPS, and all the other cool stuff… Obviously, I never got that far.

The only options I had were shitty premade engines, Unity, Unreal Engine, and alike. Sure, the last two are very good options, but they’re not free as in beer or freedom. They go against all my values.

Our Lord, our Savior

Meet Godot 4, the newest release of this 2D/3D game engine. It’s always been said that open-source and free software is somehow worse in quality than for-profit programs. First of all, free software CAN be for-profit. Free licenses only require that you pass on the code to your buyer.

Second of all, if you check out Godot, it’s got many features that are on-par with the big players.

I don’t really feel like playing games anymore, and making them isn’t very profitable either. Don’t think that these engines can only be used for traditional “games”. Many programs require advanced graphics, and Godot is very extensible.

If you’re really grateful for freedom, fight for it.
